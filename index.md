## Professional Self-Assessment

Throughout the Computer Science program, I have developed a stronger understanding of how to design, build, and evaluate software systems. This ePortfolio represents my progression from learning foundational concepts to applying them in more structured and meaningful ways.

One of the most important skills I have developed is the ability to analyze existing systems and identify areas for improvement. Early in the program, my focus was primarily on making programs function correctly. Over time, this shifted toward designing systems that are maintainable, scalable, and aligned with best practices. This shift is demonstrated through the enhancements made across all three artifacts presented in this portfolio.

In the Algorithms and Data Structures artifact, I transitioned from a static implementation to a more dynamic system using collections. This demonstrated a deeper understanding of how data structures support scalability and efficient data management.

In the Databases artifact, I expanded from executing isolated SQL queries to building a structured relational database system using real-world data. This strengthened my understanding of database design and data analysis in practical applications.

In the Software Design and Engineering artifact, I enhanced a mobile application by improving its structure, simplifying its functionality, and refining the user interface. This process reinforced the importance of separation of concerns and user-centered design.

Throughout the program, I also developed strong problem-solving and debugging skills. Working with multiple programming languages and tools improved my ability to approach challenges from different perspectives and implement effective solutions.

Overall, this ePortfolio reflects my growth from writing simple programs to developing more complete, scalable, and maintainable systems. It demonstrates my readiness to apply these skills in a professional environment and continue learning as technology evolves.
